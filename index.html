I am a second-year PhD student at Arizona State University in the Landscape Ecology and Sustainability Lab (LESL) working under advisor Jingle Wu (center).

Broadly, the research objective for my disseration is to explore the interactions between landscape pattern, climate change, and ecosystem services. A recent study estimates that land use change has occured on one third of the global land surface since the 1960's. In addition, the most recent IPCC report estimates that greenhouse gas emissions by humans have caused ~1.0C of global warming compared to pre-industrial temperatures. We know that both environmental changes are rearranging earth's ecosystems, resulting in changes to the goods and services we receive from ecosystems (e.g., food, freshwater, cultural ties to nature). To preserve ecosystem services, we must not only understand the direct effects of climate change and landscape pattern on ecosystem services, but also the interactions between landscape pattern, climate change, and ecosystem services.
